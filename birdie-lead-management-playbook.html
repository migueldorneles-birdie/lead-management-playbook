Lead Management Playbook

The single source of truth for how Birdie captures, stages, segments, nurtures, and converts leads — from first touch to closed deal.

Owner: Marketing Status: Active v1.2 Last updated: June 2026 SSOT: Airtable

🎯 Purpose & Scope

Why this document exists and what problems it solves.

For too long, contacts, leads, and sales motions were managed in silos with no consistent workflow, no single source of truth, and no shared language between Marketing and Sales. This playbook fixes that.

It defines the full lifecycle from anonymous contact to closed customer, establishes the rules of engagement at every stage, and gives Marketing and Sales a shared operating model. It should be used as the reference document for all lead-related decisions, campaign targeting, and pipeline conversations.

What this playbook enables

✅ Clarity

  • Exact definitions for Lead, MQL, SAL, SQL, SQO
  • ICP criteria locked in writing
  • Shared language between Mkt & Sales

📊 Measurement

  • Pipeline quality & ICP fit by source
  • Channel ROI and velocity tracking
  • Coverage gaps (leads with no follow-up)

🎯 Segmentation

  • By region, stage, source, segment
  • Three core lead groups with different workflows
  • Target Account separation for ABM

⚡ Activation

  • Campaign strategy per group, per month
  • Right tool for cold, warm, hot outreach
  • Domain health & deliverability protected

📈 Lead Stage Definitions

The five stages every lead passes through from capture to opportunity.

Stage 1
Lead
Any captured contact — event attendee lists, booth scans, form submissions. No qualification required.
All contacts
Stage 2
MQL
ICP fit AND at least one relevant/active engagement (form sub, booth scan, etc.) in the past 45 days.
Marketing-qualified
Stage 3
SAL
ICP fit AND shows intent (pricing page visit, two-pager download, etc.) — needs sales work to schedule; not yet a hand raise.
Sales-accepted
Stage 4
SQL
ICP fit AND hand raise (demo request) OR strong intent signal. Ready for immediate sales engagement.
Sales-qualified
Stage 5
SQO
Any SQL that becomes a formal opportunity because there is some element of BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) validated.
Opportunity
⚠️ Edge case — non-ICP hand raises: Leads that are not ICP fit but have raised their hand (demo request) will, for now, be taken by Sales for a conversation. If a validated opportunity is confirmed by the Sales Director, it counts towards the goal.

Key Definitions

Term Definition Notes
Hand Raise A demo request — the lead has explicitly asked to speak with Sales Required for SQL (along with ICP fit)
Engaged At least 4 active interactions (post likes, email opens, etc. — no form submissions) in the past 45 days Used to determine when ICP fit leads warrant Sales coordination
Intent Signal Pricing page visit, two-pager download, high-value content download Triggers SAL status when combined with ICP fit
BANT Budget · Authority · Need · Timeline — at least partial validation required for SQO Sales responsibility to confirm

🎯 ICP Criteria

A lead is ICP fit when it meets all of the following criteria. These gates determine MQL eligibility and campaign priority.

🏢 Company Size

  • Typically 200–5,000 employees
  • Large enough to have structured CX/QA teams
  • Small enough to move fast on a decision

🏭 Industry

  • FinTech / FinServ primary
  • Services Marketplaces primary
  • InsurTech · SaaS / Tech · Retail / E-commerce
  • Other verticals with active CX/QA needs
  • All verticals are MQL-eligible — primary verticals get priority campaign investment

🌍 Geography

  • US / Canada primary
  • Brazil / LATAM primary
  • Europe · UK · Other regions
  • Secondary regions still qualify as MQLs if all other criteria are met

👤 Persona / Job Title

  • CX · Customer Experience
  • Product · Insights · Intelligence
  • Operations · Quality · Support
  • Minimum level: Coordinator, Head, Manager
  • Also: VP, Director, C-suite in smaller orgs
📋 Reference: For the full Target Account list with specific companies and personas, refer to the Target Accounts documentation. The list is the ultimate arbiter of TA status — company size/industry are proxies for unverified leads.

👥 Lead Groups

Every lead falls into one of three core groups. Groups determine campaign type, communication cadence, and Sales coordination rules.

💙 ICP Fit

  • Regular mkt emails / campaigns
  • If engaged*: coordinate strategy with Sales for warm handoff
  • Goal: move to MQL → SAL → SQL

💜 Target Accounts

  • Regular mkt emails + premium campaigns
  • Premium: dinners, giveaways, free conference tickets
  • If engaged*: coordinate strategy with Sales
  • ABM-style treatment — highest priority

⬜ No ICP Fit

  • Regular mkt emails / campaigns only
  • No Sales coordination (unless validated by Sales Director)
  • Monitor for ICP re-qualification over time
*Engaged = at least 4 active interactions (post likes, email opens, etc. — form submissions excluded) in the past 45 days.

🚩 Competitor & Partner Flagging

Not every contact in our database is a prospective customer. Competitors and partners need to be identified, tagged, and routed through a different (or no) campaign track — to protect conversion metrics and avoid awkward outreach.

Contact Type Definitions

Type Who They Are Examples
Competitor Companies building a similar or adjacent product that competes directly or indirectly with Birdie in the CX intelligence / QA space Other conversation analytics platforms, QA tools, VoC software vendors
Partner Companies that integrate with Birdie, resell it, refer clients, or operate in a complementary space — they are not a buying prospect but can generate pipeline indirectly CX consulting firms, BPO/outsourcing providers, CRM or helpdesk integration partners, tech ecosystem partners

Flagging Workflow

1

Identify at Capture

When importing or capturing a new contact, check the company domain and LinkedIn profile. If the company is a known competitor or partner, set the Contact Type Flag field immediately. Don't wait for a campaign to send to them first.

2

Monthly Database Audit

During the monthly list hygiene pass, review contacts from verticals adjacent to ours (analytics, QA tools, CX consulting) and cross-check against a maintained Competitor & Partner reference list. Flag any new entries. This list lives in Airtable alongside the lead base.

3

Suppress from Active Scoring

Tagged contacts must be excluded from ICP scoring, MQL counting, and pipeline reporting. A competitor or partner booking a demo does not count as an MQL — it distorts quality metrics. Update the ICP Fit field to N and Lead Stage to Flagged.

Campaign Permissions by Flag

Campaign Type Partner Competitor Rationale
Monthly newsletter ✅ Yes ⛔ No Partners can stay informed of Birdie's work; competitors should not get product/market intelligence for free
Regular nurture emails ✅ Yes ⛔ No Partners are valid relationship targets; competitor nurturing wastes list health and signals
Premium campaigns (dinners, events, giveaways) ⛔ No ⛔ No Premium budget is reserved for ICP conversion — neither partners nor competitors should receive it
LinkedIn Ads retargeting ⚠️ OK if not excluded from audience ⛔ Exclude Add competitor domains to LinkedIn Ads exclusion lists to avoid wasted spend
Warm HubSpot sequences ⚠️ Only for partnership conversations ⛔ No Partners may receive a specific "partnership track" sequence if there's an active relationship motion
MQL / pipeline counting ⛔ Never ⛔ Never Flagged contacts must never inflate MQL or SQO numbers regardless of their actions
⚠️ Edge case — competitor employee who genuinely wants to buy: If someone at a competitor company reaches out personally (e.g. they're evaluating Birdie for a side project or a new role), this should be flagged for Sales Director review before proceeding. Don't auto-suppress individuals just because of employer domain.
📌 Airtable setup: Create a dedicated Competitors & Partners reference table with company domains. Run a lookup/formula against the main leads base to auto-suggest flags on import. Manual confirmation is still required before the flag is set as final.

💚 Customers & Evangelists

Active users are managed separately — they are not in the lead CRM but need their own campaign strategy.

🟢 Customer

  • Definition: active Birdie user
  • Products used: VoC, QA
  • Database: lives in the app (not HubSpot — scale is too large, e.g. 6k+ at Nubank alone)
  • Campaigns: delivered via the tool Product already uses for feature announcements, events, etc.

🟠 Evangelist

  • Definition: active Birdie user AND promoter
  • Flagged in app database
  • Activate for case studies, referrals, G2 reviews, speaking slots
  • High-value for ABM social proof
📌 Architecture note: The customer base in the app is separate from the lead CRM. Only flag customers as leads if there is an expansion/upsell motion — coordinate with Product on email tooling to avoid double-sending.

⚙️ Data Enrichment

How Birdie enriches contact and company data across the lead lifecycle — which tools cover what, when enrichment runs, what fields gate each stage, and how enriched data drives routing decisions.

Enrichment Sources

Source Coverage
Apollo Company size · Industry · Tech stack · Job title · Seniority · Direct email · LinkedIn URL. Runs automatically on every net-new contact entering Airtable. Default tool for manual enrichment. Brazil gap: use Speedio as fallback when Apollo returns incomplete results.
RB2B Company name · Visitor LinkedIn profile · Page visited · Visit timestamp. Identifies anonymous website visitors and creates net-new contacts in Airtable or updates intent signal on existing ones.
LinkedIn Ads Ad engagement type · Campaign source · Job title · Company name (from lead gen form fills). Flows into HubSpot via native integration and elevates contact score.
HubSpot Sequence engagement · Email replies · Meeting booked · Deal stage · Owner assignment. Becomes the primary enrichment source at MQL+; Airtable mirrors key fields via sync.
Speedio (proposed) CNPJ-based company lookup and direct contact data for Brazilian prospects. Recommended for Brazil MKT queue when Apollo coverage is thin.
ℹ️ Mailchimp and Warmup Inbox are not enrichment sources. Email engagement signals (opens, clicks, bounces) feed contact health scoring — covered in CRM Health → Ongoing Hygiene Rules.

Enrichment Triggers

Enrichment runs in two tiers to optimise credit usage. Tier 1 is lightweight and runs on all contacts to determine ICP fit. Tier 2 is full enrichment and runs only on contacts that pass Tier 1. No ICP Fit contacts skip Tier 2 entirely and enter a lightweight monthly maintenance routine instead.

Tier 1 — Qualification enrichment (all contacts)

  • Company name · Company domain
  • Industry
  • Company size

Tier 2 — Full enrichment (ICP Fit & Target Account only)

  • Job title
  • Direct email
  • LinkedIn URL
  • Tech stack
Trigger When Action Owner
On entry — Tier 1 Net-new contact added to Airtable Apollo enriches company-level fields only. ICP fit is evaluated. Global MKT
On entry — Tier 2 Tier 1 confirms ICP Fit or Target Account Apollo runs full contact enrichment. Contact is scored, lead group assigned, and routed. Global MKT
On intent signal RB2B detects a website visit Contact is created or updated with visit data. Tier 1 runs if not yet ICP-qualified. Tier 2 runs if ICP Fit is confirmed. Global MKT
On stage change Contact advances to next stage Required fields for the target stage are validated. If fields are missing, Tier 2 runs before the transition is confirmed. Global MKT · Brazil MKT
Monthly maintenance Contact is in No ICP Fit group Lightweight check for job or company change only. If a change is detected that may affect ICP fit, Tier 1 re-runs. No full enrichment unless ICP fit is confirmed. Global MKT · Brazil MKT
Quarterly refresh ICP Fit or Target Account contact with no stage movement or engagement for 90+ days Tier 2 re-enrichment to catch role or company changes. Contact is re-scored. Global MKT · Brazil MKT

Required Fields by Stage

These fields must be populated before a contact can advance to the next stage. Tier 1 fields gate the ICP fit decision; Tier 2 fields gate activation.

Stage Required Fields
Lead Full name · Email · Company name · Lead source
MQL Industry · Company size (Tier 1 — ICP fit confirmed) · Job title · Region · Direct email (non-generic) · LinkedIn URL · Lead group assigned · At least 1 engagement signal (Tier 2 — full enrichment required)
SAL ICP score confirmed · Competitor/partner flag checked · Lead group reviewed and accepted by Sales
SQL Contact exists in HubSpot · Need identified · Authority confirmed · Budget signal · Timeline indicated (BANT fields captured in HubSpot and synced back to Airtable)
SQO Full BANT validated · Deal created in HubSpot · Owner assigned · Expected close date set
Job title as proxy: Job title is the proxy for persona and seniority fit at MQL. If the title doesn't map to a qualifying persona (CX, Product, Operations, Quality, Support at min. Coordinator/Manager/Head level), the contact does not advance regardless of other signals.
HubSpot handoff: From SQL onwards, HubSpot is the system of record. Airtable reflects key fields via sync but Sales owns data entry at this stage.

Routing Rules

Enriched data drives three routing decisions: lead group assignment, regional queue split, and competitor/partner flagging. These are evaluated at entry and re-evaluated at every stage change.

Rule Condition Output
Target Account Company matches ABM target list Lead group = Target Account · Flagged for premium campaigns (LinkedIn Ads, Luma events)
ICP Fit — Nurture ICP criteria met · No engagement signal Lead group = ICP Fit · Enters nurture sequence · Stays in Marketing queue
ICP Fit — MQL ICP criteria met · At least 1 engagement signal Lead group = ICP Fit · Flagged as MQL · Routed to Sales review queue for SAL decision
No ICP Fit Any ICP criterion fails Lead group = No ICP Fit · Cold/bulk activation only · No warm sequence
Brazil queue Region = Brazil/LATAM Routed to Brazil MKT queue · Activated via Brazil-specific cadences
Global queue Region = US/Canada or other Routed to Global MKT queue · Activated via global cadences
Competitor/partner flag Company domain matches exclusion list Removed from all activation queues · Moved to Competitors & Partners table in Airtable
Routing is dynamic: If enrichment updates a field that changes ICP fit (e.g. company growth crosses 200 headcount, or job title changes to a qualifying persona), the contact is re-evaluated and re-routed automatically.
ICP Fit — Nurture vs. MQL: The distinction is purely driven by engagement. The same contact can move between the two states automatically as engagement signals are updated. Lead group and queue assignment live in Airtable. HubSpot reflects the assigned owner from SQL onwards.

🛠️ Tool Stack by Outreach Heat

Match the right tool to the right lead temperature. Mixing these breaks deliverability.

❄️ Cold

Mailchimp

Domain: fly-birdie.com · Sender: pat@
Use for: bulk cold campaigns, unengaged No ICP leads. Max 3 emails per sequence. Suppress after 3×no-open.

❄️ Cold

LinkedIn Ads

Always-on TA targeting. Retargeting for website visitors and content engagers. Supports ABM at scale without domain risk.

🌡️ Warm

HubSpot Sequences

Domain: usebirdie.com · Senders: pat@, saiago@
Use for: contacts with a signal — opened email, attended webinar, form fill, LinkedIn engagement.

🌡️ Warm

LinkedIn DMs

Use for: engaged ICP/TA contacts where a personal connection makes sense. Reference the signal. Keep it short — no decks, no walls of text.

🔥 Hot

HubSpot CRM Deal

Domain: birdie.ai or personal
Use for: SQL+ contacts. Active deal in HubSpot. Sales owns. Marketing supports with targeted content and case studies.

🌡️ Events

Luma (Premium)

Use for: premium TA campaigns — dinners, roundtables, exclusive events. Luma Premium if scale requires. Registrants auto-enroll in warm HubSpot sequence.

📌 Important: Never use birdie.ai for marketing campaigns — it's transactional only. Mixing marketing volume into the main domain will damage deliverability for product and deal emails.

🔥 fly-birdie.com Warm-up Timeline

Complete this 4-week warm-up before sending any real cold campaign from fly-birdie.com. Skipping this step risks the entire domain being blacklisted.

Week 1
10–20 /day

100% automated Warmup Inbox traffic. No real sends. Let reputation build from zero.

Week 2
30–50 /day

Introduce a small batch of your most-engaged contacts — past event registrants, known email openers.

Week 3
70–100 /day

Begin soft campaigns — newsletter-style content, no hard CTA. Measure open rate & spam rate.

Week 4
150+ /day

Domain is ready for real cold volume. Start 3-email cold cadence. Monitor Google Postmaster daily.

Pre-send Config Checklist

  • SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured for fly-birdie.com
  • Warmup Inbox pool active and running 24/7 in background
  • Unsubscribe link present in every Mailchimp template
  • Suppression list synced between Mailchimp ↔ HubSpot
  • Google Postmaster set up and monitoring spam rate (target <0.1%)
  • Hard bounce suppression enabled in Mailchimp

🧹 Ongoing CRM Hygiene Rules

Non-negotiables for keeping domain health high and list quality sharp.

  • Warmup Inbox stays on permanently for fly-birdie.com — do not turn it off between campaigns. It maintains the domain's reputation baseline.
  • Monthly list hygiene: remove contacts with 3+ sends and zero engagement from active lists. Move to a suppressed "unresponsive" segment.
  • Keep cold send volume under 200/day per mailbox until domain reputation is fully established (typically 60–90 days after initial warm-up).
  • Watch spam rate in Google Postmaster (for Gmail-heavy segments) — keep below 0.1%. Above 0.3% = pause and audit.
  • Never re-send to hard bounces or contacts who have received 3+ sends with no engagement.
  • Suppression sync: any unsubscribe in Mailchimp must propagate to HubSpot within 24 hours (use native integration or Zapier).
  • GDPR compliance: ensure consent status is tracked as a field in Airtable / HubSpot for all EU contacts.

Cold Email Cadence (fly-birdie.com / Mailchimp)

Email 1 · Day 0

Value-first, no pitch

A stat, a POV, or a short insight relevant to their vertical. Goal: open + click.

Email 2 · Day 7

Light social proof

A customer result or a quote. Soft CTA — "worth a look?" — no pressure.

Email 3 · Day 17

Direct ask

One clear ask, short. If no engagement after this, suppress. Done.

Warm Sequence (usebirdie.com / HubSpot)

Day 0

Personalised intro

Reference the signal: "saw you joined our CX Stack Reset webinar…"

Day 3

Value add

A relevant resource, case study, or a genuinely useful question.

Day 7

Soft ask

Short ask for a conversation. No decks, no walls of text. One sentence ask.

Day 14

Final follow-up

"Leaving this here if timing changes." Short. No pressure. Exit sequence after no reply.

📊 Strategic KPIs

The questions this playbook should allow us to answer at any time. If we can't answer these, we have a data quality problem.

🔬 Pipeline Quality & ICP Fit

  • What % of leads from each source convert to MQL?
  • Which sources generate Target Account leads vs. noise?
  • What's our TA lead rate overall and by source?
  • Do cold outreach leads convert differently than inbound?

💰 Channel ROI & Prioritisation

  • Which channels should we double down on vs. cut in Q3?
  • Are webinar leads converting to MQL at higher rates?
  • What is the cost-per-MQL by channel?

🎯 ICP & Segment Validation

  • Are FinTech/InsurTech leads converting at higher rates?
  • Which company sizes appear most in TA leads?
  • Are we reaching CX/Insights personas, or drifting adjacent?

⚡ Velocity & Coverage

  • How many MQLs/week? Are we on pace for quarterly targets?
  • What's the follow-up lag from capture to first touch?
  • How many TA leads have no activity logged? (coverage gaps)

👩‍💼 Roles & Responsibilities

Who owns what. Clarity here prevents duplicated work and missed handoffs.

Global Marketing Lead

Global Marketing Lead

  • Defines ICP criteria, stage definitions, and lead segmentation
  • Sets brand-level campaign strategy per group per month — awareness, nurture, and conversion campaigns
  • Owns campaigns tied to GTM positioning, demand generation, and pipeline growth
  • Oversees Airtable SSOT structure and data integrity
  • Reviews sprint metrics and adjusts priorities
  • Coordinates with Sales Director on non-ICP hand raises
BDR / MKT Ops · Brazil

BDR / MKT Ops · Brazil

  • Executes campaigns while automation is not yet live
  • Generates and manages Brazil lead lists
  • Operates MKT/Sales motions for Brazil region
  • BDR function: first touch, list building, list hygiene
  • Transitions to primarily BDR once automation is live
Product Marketing & Automation

Product Marketing & Automation

  • Owns all product-focused communications — feature announcements, release updates, product-led campaigns, and in-product email moments
  • Plans and executes product marketing campaigns end-to-end, separate from brand/demand campaigns owned by Miguel
  • Automates campaign execution from mapped workflows
  • Sets up HubSpot enrollment triggers and lifecycle automations
  • Maintains Mailchimp ↔ HubSpot sync and Airtable automations
Sales

Sales Team

  • Accepts SAL/SQL handoffs from Marketing
  • Validates BANT for SQO conversion
  • Confirms non-ICP hand raises with Sales Director
  • Logs all activity in HubSpot CRM (no dark ops)
  • Gives feedback on lead quality weekly

If yes, Global Marketing Lead leads. Does it require product roadmap knowledge or in-app context? If yes, Product Marketing & Automation leads.
